1.Collect Some Shellfishes


Murex brandaris and Thais haemastoma, native to the Mediterranean, are specieses of medium-sized predatory sea snail, an edible marine gastropod mollusk in the family Muricidae, the murex snails or the rock snails. To make the dye, we need both of these shells.











2.Break the Shell


Use a hammer to crack open the shell of one of these spiky, carnivorous gastropods, you will see a pale hypobrachial gland or “bloom” transecting its body.
















3. Squeeze the Liquid Out


Take out the gland, a bulb full of liquid. Squeeze it, and then a single drop of clear liquid, smelling of garlic, will be released. Within moments, the sunlight will turn the liquid first pale yellow, then sea green, then blue, and finally a dark purple-red.














4.Dissolve the Liquid in Water


Take all the semi-liquid parts taken from two kinds of shellfishes, and put them into a cup of water. Use a stick to stir it until it is fully mixed and turns into a yellow liquid.















5.Left It in the Sun


Left the liquid in the sun for a couple of minutes. Then you can put some cloth into the liquid to get it dyed. It will take a few minutes for the cloth to turn into a tyrian purple.














6. Get Tyrian Purple


Experiment with different portions of the liquid from two shellfishes or different duration under the sun, you will get different shades of purple and blue.
